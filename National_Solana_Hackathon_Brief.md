## **CASE 1\. Tokenization of Real-World Assets (RWA)**

### **1\. Context**

The world of finance and ownership is gradually becoming digital.  
However, real-world assets such as real estate, commodities, bonds, and energy still remain:

* difficult for the broader public to access  
* difficult to manage and transfer  
* opaque and not always liquid

Tokenization makes it possible to convert real-world assets into digital tokens and open global access to them.

### **2\. Problem Space**

Within this track, participants may address one or more of the following problems:

* High entry barriers, for example, buying real estate or bonds often requires large amounts of capital  
* Low liquidity of real-world assets  
* Lack of transparency in ownership and transactions  
* Complex accounting and income distribution processes, such as rent or dividends  
* Lack of global access, for example, local markets with a limited audience  
* Distrust of intermediaries

***NOTE:** You do not need to solve every problem. Choose a focus and build around it.*

### **3\. Task**

Build a product on Solana that does one or more of the following:

* tokenize real-world assets, for example: real estate, bonds, commodities, or energy, with fractional ownership and secondary trading  
* manage ownership, transfer, or yield through blockchain  
* simplify access to investing or asset management  
* The solution must demonstrate a full end-to-end user flow, including issuance, ownership, and interaction with the asset.

This may be either infrastructure, such as a protocol, or an end-user product, such as a platform or application.

### **4\. Possible Solution Formats**

You may build:

* A fractional real-estate ownership platform  
* An energy tokenization system, for example, P2P electricity trading  
* Tokenized bonds or financial instruments  
* A tokenization platform or asset issuance builder  
* Solutions for ownership accounting and income distribution  
* A marketplace for trading tokenized assets  
* Infrastructure, such as protocols, SDKs, or developer tools  
* Solutions that connect the physical asset with its digital representation, such as an oracle or proof-of-asset layer

***NOTE:** You may go beyond these examples if the solution still fits the theme.*

### **5\. Minimum Requirements (MVP)**

Your solution must:

* use the Solana blockchain  
* include a deployed smart contract (`program`)  
* allow:  
  * token creation / issuance  
  * ownership or transaction management  
  * a basic interface (`web / bot / demo`)  
  * a connection between the real-world asset and the token, for example: an oracle, off-chain data, or another verification mechanism

### **6\. Restrictions**

❌ You may not submit only an idea or a slide deck without a prototype  
❌ You may not rely only on off-chain logic without using Solana  
❌ You may not build “pseudo-Web3” solutions without real token usage

### **7\. What Matters in the Presentation and Demo**

In the presentation and demo, show:

* what problem you solve  
* how the product works, including the user flow  
* how Solana is used in a meaningful way, not just formally  
* the architecture, briefly  
* a demo or working prototype  
* scaling potential  
* submission on [colosseum.com](http://colosseum.com) (submission opens on April 7, 2026)

## **CASE 2\. AI \+ Blockchain: Autonomous Smart Contracts**

### **1\. Context**

Blockchain provides transparency and immutability, but smart contracts remain rigidly programmed:

* they do not adapt to changing conditions  
* they do not make complex decisions  
* they do not work with unstructured data

On the other hand, AI can analyze data, learn, and make decisions,  
but it does not inherently provide transparency or trust.

Combining AI and blockchain creates the possibility of autonomous systems that:

* make decisions  
* manage assets  
* act according to predefined logic  
* remain verifiable on-chain

### **2\. Problem Space**

You may address one or more of the following problems:

* Smart contracts do not adapt to changing conditions such as markets or user behavior  
* Decisions require manual management or centralized control  
* There is no transparent way to use AI in financial or decentralized systems  
* It is difficult to trust AI when its decisions are opaque  
* There are not enough tools for automatic management of DeFi, assets, or operational processes

Your task is to show how AI can become part of on-chain logic.

### **3\. Hackathon Task**

Build a product on Solana in which:

* AI participates in decision-making  
* those decisions lead to smart-contract state changes through on-chain actions  
* the system operates semi-autonomously or fully autonomously

This may be:

* an AI agent  
* an autonomous protocol  
* an asset-management system  
* infrastructure for AI + Web3

### **4\. Possible Solution Formats**

You may build:

* an AI agent that manages assets such as trading, allocation, or risk management  
* a DeFi protocol with dynamic parameters such as rates, fees, or limits  
* a DAO with an AI assistant for governance decisions  
* a system for data analysis and automatic on-chain actions  
* autonomous services, for example insurance, lending, or income distribution  
* infrastructure for integrating AI with smart contracts

You may go beyond these examples, as long as AI genuinely affects the system logic.

### **5\. Minimum Requirements (MVP)**

Your solution must:

* use the Solana blockchain  
* include a deployed smart contract (`program`)  
* include an AI component such as a model, API, or agent  
* demonstrate the chain:  
* AI → decision → on-chain transaction → smart-contract state change  
* include a demo such as a web app, bot, or usage scenario

### **6\. Restrictions**

❌ You may not build “AI for show” that does not affect logic  
❌ You may not build purely off-chain AI without contract integration  
❌ You may not submit a static contract without adaptivity  
❌ You may not stop at AI recommendations without actual on-chain execution

Simplified models such as APIs or mocks are allowed if the autonomy logic is clearly demonstrated.

### **7\. What Matters in the Presentation and Demo**

In the presentation and demo, show:

* what task the system solves  
* how the AI makes decisions  
* how those decisions affect the smart contract  
* the system's level of autonomy  
* the architecture, including the AI + blockchain connection  
* how AI initiates or influences on-chain transactions  
* submission on [colosseum.com](http://colosseum.com) (submission opens on April 7, 2026)

### **7. Additional Advantages**

Additional points may be awarded to solutions that:  
• use real-world scenarios such as finance, real estate, or energy  
• demonstrate relevance to the real economy  
• integrate with existing infrastructure such as exchanges or payment systems
